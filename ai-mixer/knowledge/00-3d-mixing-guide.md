# The Producer's 3D Mixing Guide: UKG, House, & Trap

## I. The Foundation: Low-End Management

In these genres, the low-end isn't just a sound; it's the physical energy.

### The Trap 808/Kick Relationship
- **Sidechain is King**: Use a fast-release sidechain on the 808 triggered by the Kick. The Kick provides the click (transient), and the 808 provides the body (sustain).
- **Clipping over Limiting**: For Trap, use a Soft Clipper on your master or drum bus. It allows the peaks to hit "into the red" for that aggressive saturation without the pumping artifacts of a limiter.

### The UK Garage "Pluck" Bass
- **Sub vs. Mid-Bass**: Layer a clean sine wave for sub-frequencies with a triangle or saw wave (cut below 100Hz) for the "pop" that translates on laptop speakers.
- **Envelope Shaping**: Use a fast attack and a medium-short decay to ensure the bass "interlocks" with the shuffled hi-hats rather than washing over them.

### The House "Four-on-the-Floor" Anchor
- **Mono Compatibility**: Ensure all content below 120Hz is strictly mono. Stereo sub-information creates phase cancellation that kills the impact in a club.

## II. The 3D Space: Width, Height, and Depth

To achieve a "pro" sound, you must visualize your mix as a physical room.

### 1. Height (Frequency Distribution)
- **High-Pass Everything**: If it's not a Kick or Bass, high-pass it. Even a small amount of low-end mud in a vocal or synth can steal "headroom" from your sub.
- **The "Air" Band**: For Garage and House vocals, use a gentle high-shelf boost around 12 kHz to add "shimmer" and "expensive" texture.

### 2. Width (The Stereo Stage)
- **The Mid-Side Strategy**: Keep your lead vocal and snare in the "Mid" (center). Use Mid-Side EQ to boost the "Sides" of your pads and background vocal chops above 5 kHz to make the mix feel wrap-around.
- **UKG Percussion**: Pan your shuffled hats and rimshots slightly (e.g., 10-15% Left/Right) to mimic the movement of a live drummer and prevent a "static" center.

### 3. Depth (The Z-Axis: Reverb & Delay)
- **The Pre-Delay Trick**: If you want a vocal to sound close but still have a huge reverb, increase the Pre-Delay (20-40ms). This allows the dry vocal to hit the listener's ear before the reverb starts, keeping it "forward" in the 3D space.
- **The Distance Rule**:
  - Close (Forward): High volume, high high-end, long pre-delay, dry.
  - Far (Back): Lower volume, low-pass filtered (less highs), 0ms pre-delay, wet.
- **Send, Don't Insert**: Always put your Reverbs and Delays on Return Tracks. This lets you EQ the reverb itself (e.g., cutting the lows of the reverb) so it doesn't muddy up your dry instruments.

## III. Genre-Specific "Secret Sauce"

| Feature | UK Garage | House | Trap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Drums | 60-65% Swing; vary velocity on every 16th note. | Rigid 4/4; use "Glue Compression" on the bus. | Hard-panned rolls; soft-clipped Master. |
| Vocals | Pitch up +3 to +5 semitones; heavy de-essing. | Lush plates; "ducked" reverb (sidechained to vocal). | Autotune; crisp 8 kHz boost; short delays. |
| Transitions | Fast cuts, spinbacks, and vocal stutters. | Long, filtered blends (8-16 bars). | Impact risers, "tape stops," and sub-drops. |

## IV. Final Polish: The "Ear Test"

- **The Mono Check**: Regularly collapse your mix to mono. If your House piano or Trap synth disappears, you have phase issues.
- **Reference Tracks**: Drag a professional track (e.g. a Sammy Virji or Metro Boomin track) into your session. Gain-match it to your mix and compare the "weight" of the low-end.
